Attention Mechanisms Compared

Reference output bundle — query: "How do different attention mechanisms compare in terms of complexity and performance?"

This report compares three attention mechanisms used in transformer architectures: scaled dot-product attention, multi-head attention, and cross-attention.

Scaled Dot-Product Attention

Attention(Q, K, V) = softmax(QK^T / sqrt(d_k)) V
  • Complexity: O(n² · d) where n is sequence length and d is dimension.
  • Parallelizable: Fully, unlike recurrence.

Multi-Head Attention

Runs h parallel attention heads on d/h-dimensional projections, then concatenates.

  • Complexity: Same asymptotic as single-head (O(n² · d)), but allows attending to different representation subspaces.
  • Typical config: h=8, d_model=512 → d_k = d_v = 64 per head.

Cross-Attention (Encoder-Decoder)

Queries come from the decoder, keys and values from the encoder output. Same mechanism, different source for K and V.
